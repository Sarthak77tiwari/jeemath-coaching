Q.01 📐 Trigonometry Class 11 · NCERT Ch.3
Prove that:

cos 3x = 4cos³x − 3cosx

Then find the exact value of:
cos 20° × cos 40° × cos 80° = ?
💡 Hint: Expand cos(2x + x) using addition formula. For the product — substitute x = 20° into the identity you just proved.
Medium · Board + JEE favourite
cos 20° × cos 40° × cos 80° = 1/8
Multiply both sides by sin 20° and use sin 3θ = 3sinθ − 4sin³θ
This exact product question appeared in JEE Mains 2023. We cover the full method in Week 1 of the program.
Q.02 📈 Sequences & Series Class 11 · NCERT Ch.9
The sum of the first n terms of a series is:

Sn = 3n² + 5n

Find the nth term Tn.
Is the series an AP? If yes, find first term and common difference.
💡 Hint: Tn = Sn − Sn−1 for n ≥ 2. Always check T1 = S1 separately.
Medium · Appears every year in boards
Tn = 6n + 2  ·  Yes, AP with a = 8, d = 6
This Sn → Tn shortcut saves 2 mins in the exam. Taught in Week 2.
Most students try to guess the formula directly and lose all 4 marks. We teach the guaranteed method.
Q.03 ∫ Integration Class 12 · NCERT Ch.7
Evaluate without a calculator:

∫ x²x² + 4x + 3 dx

Show every step.
💡 Hint: Numerator degree = denominator degree. You must do polynomial long division first — before any partial fractions.
Hard · Most students get this wrong
x − 52ln|x+1| + 32ln|x+3| + C
Skipping the long division step is the #1 mistake. It costs full marks.
Over 60% of Class 12 students write a wrong answer here by skipping Step 1. We drill the correct order in Week 5.
Q.04 🔢 Matrices Class 12 · NCERT Ch.3
If A = ⎡ 2   3 ⎤
⎣ 1   2 ⎦

Find A⁻¹ and verify A · A⁻¹ = I

Then use A⁻¹ to solve: 2x + 3y = 5  and  x + 2y = 4
💡 Hint: A⁻¹ = (1/det A) × adj A. If det(A) = 0, inverse doesn't exist — always check this first.
Hard · 5-mark board question type
A⁻¹ = ⎡ 2  −3 ⎤ / ⎣ −1   2 ⎦  ·  Solution: x = −2, y = 3
The "use matrix to solve equations" method is a 5-mark guaranteed question in Class 12 boards. Covered fully in Week 3.
Q.05 📉 App. of Derivatives Class 12 · NCERT Ch.6
Find the intervals where

f(x) = x³ − 3x² + 4

is strictly increasing. Also find local maxima and minima with values.
💡 Hint: Find f′(x), set it to zero for critical points. Then test the sign of f′(x) in each interval — or use the second derivative test.
Hard · 6 marks in boards
Increasing on (−∞, 0) ∪ (2, ∞)
Local max at x = 0 (value = 4) · Local min at x = 2 (value = 0)
Application of Derivatives = highest-weightage chapter in Class 12 boards. We spend 2 full weeks on it.
Q.06 🎲 Probability Class 12 · NCERT Ch.13
A fair coin is tossed 5 times.

Find the probability of getting at least 3 heads.

Now the harder part: given that the first toss was heads, what's the probability of getting at least 3 heads total?
💡 Hint: Part 1 uses Binomial Distribution B(5, 1/2). Part 2 uses conditional probability — Bayes is not needed, just careful sample space reduction.
Hard · Conditional probability twist
At least 3 heads: 1/2  ·  Given first = H: 11/16
Part 2 catches almost everyone — the sample space changes from 32 to 16 outcomes.
Conditional probability is the most common 4-mark question in Class 12 boards. Taught with 6 different question types in Week 7.
